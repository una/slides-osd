Defining "Open Source Design"

Designing In The Open
Showing design progress in the open, or "designing out loud."
Designing Free Resources
Creating resources that are free for others.
Designing for Open Source Projects
Working in conjunction with developers on open source projects.

Goal: Foster Design Participation in the Open Source Community

  1. Increase understanding of the importance of design
  2. Improve communication lapses
  3. Provide easy open source project discovery
  4. Provide easy designer discovery
  5. Figure out a lucid workflow for everyone

Lower the Barrier to Entry for Designers

Barrier 1: The "Fuck You, Pay Me" Mentality

Uhm... you want me to do free work?

Designers often feel undervalued for their craft and therefore have a knee-jerk reaction when asked to do free work.

But really, "free work" just sounds awful.

Barrier 2: The Museum Effect

I don't want to break everything

When you're unfamiliar with a system (like Github) every action feels like it could be potentially disastrous. You don't want to break everything so you don't touch anything.

Barrier 3: Assumed Experience

Wait, so what's a pull request?

Github is not generally used as a design tool. Developers forget that it's not the most intuitive system.

Barrier 4: The Threat of "Design By Committee"

I just don't want other people dictating my designs. That's a nightmare

Barrier 5: Unfamiliarity Paralysis

So, I want to help out... but how do I get started?

When there is an overwhelming amount to learn, it's not very inviting and the incentive to participate decreases.
